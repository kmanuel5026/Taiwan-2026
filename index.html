Festivals & Events During Your Dates

November is one of the best months to visit Taiwan — dry, warm in the south, and stacked with cultural events. Your dates overlap with two major events perfectly: the Golden Horse Film Festival (Taiwan's equivalent of the Oscars for Chinese-language cinema) runs the entire time you're in Taipei, and the Tainan Jazz Festival lands exactly as you arrive in Tainan.

🎬 Golden Horse Film Festival
Nov 6–22, 2026 · Taipei cinemas
Taiwan's most prestigious film event — the Oscars of Chinese-language cinema. Screenings across Taipei include the recurring Queer Utopia programme and international premieres. Book tickets at goldenhorse.org.tw about 2 weeks before. Evening screenings are easy to slot around sightseeing.
🎷 Tainan Jazz Festival
Nov 7–8, 2026 · Wu Garden, West Central District, Tainan
Outdoor multi-stage jazz festival in the heart of Tainan's historic district, walking distance from your hotel. Local and international acts, free or low-cost entry, social atmosphere. You arrive the evening of Nov 6 — the timing couldn't be better.
🌹 Rose Exhibition
Mid-November · Xinsheng Park, Taipei
Free outdoor flower exhibition at its peak during your return to Taipei. Beautiful and unhurried — easy to fold into Day 13 alongside Guandu Temple and a Golden Horse screening.
🎭 Pas'ta'ai (Saisiyat Dwarf Spirit Ritual)
~Nov 14–15, 2026 · Miaoli / Hsinchu counties
A rare biennial indigenous ceremony held only in even years — all-night traditional songs, dancing, and millet wine. Falls just after your departure. Worth knowing about; can't realistically be added to the route but is extraordinary context for your time in Taiwan.

Before You Go — Practical Essentials

Getting Around
EasyCard — pick up at the airport on arrival. Works on every MRT, all city buses, YouBike rentals, and convenience store payments. Top up at any MRT station or 7-Eleven.

Airport MRT — direct from TPE to Taipei Main Station, 35 min, NT$160. Runs every 15 min.

HSR (High Speed Rail) — book in advance via the THSR app (in English). Taipei→Tainan 1h40m (NT$1,350), Tainan→Kaohsiung 30 min by TRA regular train (NT$69).

Kaohsiung has its own MRT + LRT light rail. The Red Line is a single straight north-south line — very easy to navigate.
Language
English signage is excellent on Taipei MRT and HSR. Most urban restaurants have picture menus or QR codes. Kaohsiung MRT is fully bilingual.

Google Translate camera mode is your best tool for menus and signs throughout — point, translate, order.

Tainan is the spottiest for English — vendors are warm and patient with pointing. A few saved phrases in Mandarin help (xiè xiè = thank you, duō shǎo qián = how much?).

Taiwan is remarkably easy to navigate without Mandarin — especially with QR codes and apps.
Weather & What to Pack
Taipei (Nov 1–6, 12–14): 18–24°C. Light jacket for evenings; mid-month can cool quickly. Low rain probability.

Tainan / Kaohsiung (Nov 7–12): 22–28°C. Essentially no rain. T-shirts and light layers.

Hiking gear: Good walking shoes are essential — you'll cover 8–15km most days. Teapot Mountain requires ankle support.

Outdoor shopping: Do this on Day 2 (Taipei) before going south — you can check purchased items in luggage.

No umbrella needed for most of the trip, but a light packable one for Taipei doesn't hurt.
City One

Taipei

November 1–6 · 5 nights · arrival 9pm Nov 1

Taiwan's capital is a city of effortless layers — volcanic mountains rising above neon-lit night markets, one of Asia's most vibrant queer scenes anchored around the Red House in Ximending, and a constellation of hiking trails that put you above the skyline in under an hour. Taipei rewards wandering: every block has a temple alley, a craft café, or a mural you didn't expect. Staying in Ximending puts you at the centre of the queer scene, two minutes from the Red House, and one MRT stop from the airport train.

Stay: Ximending district — amba Taipei Ximending (mid-range, NT$3,500–6,000) · Comma Boutique Hotel (artsy, rooftop balcony) · CityInn Hotel Plus Ximending (budget, NT$1,500–2,500) · All within 10-min walk of Red House & Ximen MRT

Day 2 · Mon Nov 2

Longshan Temple · Bopiliao · Outdoor Gear · Cinema Park · Red House

Must-add: Longshan + Bopiliao Zone: Wanhua → Ximending
7:30am
Temple
Walk to Longshan Temple (Longshan Temple MRT, Blue Line, 1 stop from Ximen — or 20-min walk through Wanhua). Arrive before 8am.
Longshan Temple 龍山寺
📍 Wanhua District 🕐 Open 6am–10pm daily 💰 Free 🚇 Longshan Temple MRT (Blue Line)
Built in 1738 by Fujian settlers, Longshan is the most important and atmospheric temple in Taipei — a syncretic space where Buddhist, Taoist, and folk deities coexist across multiple halls. Over 100 deities are enshrined, including Guanyin (goddess of mercy), Mazu (sea goddess), and the Rabbit God — the only deity in Taiwan specifically dedicated to same-sex relationships. The morning chanting ceremony at 8am is free, extraordinary, and genuinely moving: a full chorus of devotees in a haze of incense, the sound enveloping the entire courtyard. Go early for a peaceful experience before tourists arrive. The temple has been rebuilt after WWII bombing but the spiritual energy is entirely authentic.
Don't miss the Rabbit God (Tu Er Shen) shrine at the back left of the complex — the deity traditionally governing same-sex love. Particularly meaningful to visit.
Herb Alley (青草巷) runs directly adjacent — rows of traditional medicine vendors with dried roots, medicinal teas, and centuries of practice. The underground fortune teller arcade on Guangzhou St. is right below (language barrier but atmospheric).
9:30am
Heritage
Bopiliao Historical Block — 5-min walk east of the temple.
Bopiliao Historical Block 剝皮寮歷史街區
📍 5-min walk east of Longshan Temple 🕐 Tue–Sun 9am–6pm 💰 Free
Taipei's only completely intact Qing Dynasty and Japanese colonial streetscape — a row of red-brick arcades with carved windows and traditional wooden signboards, preserved exactly as they were in the 1800s. Unlike other heritage areas where modernity has crept in, Bopiliao was intentionally protected as a museum district. Small free exhibitions inside the restored buildings trace everyday Taipei life from the era of ox carts and merchant stalls. Quiet, photogenic, and takes about 45 minutes at a relaxed pace. Makes a striking contrast to the temple's living energy immediately beside it.
Best for photography in the morning light, when the brick textures glow warm. Bring a camera.
11:00am
Outdoor Shopping
MRT or 10-min walk to outdoor gear district near Taipei Main Station. The Zhongxiao E. Rd / Zhongshan N. Rd roundabout has 5–7 specialist shops within a 5-minute walk of each other — the highest concentration of outdoor gear in Taiwan. Buy here and check it into your south-bound luggage on Day 6.
Outdoor Gear Cluster · Zhongxiao / Zhongshan Roundabout
📍 5-min walk east of Taipei Main Station Exit 7 🕐 Usually 10am–9pm
100 Mountain (百岳) — the best overall shop, excellent Mammut and Fjällräven selection, staff are helpful. TPSS (臺北山水) — carries many international brands not found in other shops. Deng Shan You (登山友) — been here since 1959, incredible discount rack on the 2nd floor (Hagglöfs, RAB, Deuter at sale prices). Patagonia nearby. Taiwanese techwear brands like oqLiq and WISDOM — functional waterproof clothing designed for Taiwan's humidity, with a cyberpunk aesthetic — are found in Xinyi and Zhongshan districts if you want local design labels.
Allow 1–2 hours. Prices are competitive. The Deng Shan You discount rack is worth digging through.
2:00pm
Street Art
Taipei Cinema Park and America Street in Ximending — large-scale murals, graffiti-style public art, skate culture energy. America Street (美國街) has more artwork, vintage stores, and counterculture shops. Both are within the Ximending pedestrian zone, easy to fold into a wander.
6:00pm
Evening

Café Dalida is the cornerstone — birthplace of drag in Taipei, with an open-air terrace perfect for people-watching. If you want to dance, Taboo in Zhongshan opens Wednesday nights.
🎬 Golden Horse Film Festival
The festival opens today (Nov 6 in Taipei — you'll be on the HSR south, but screenings started). Check goldenhorse.org.tw for this evening's program at Taipei cinemas. The Queer Utopia section is worth noting for your return days.

Day 3 · Tue Nov 3

Elephant Mountain · Xinyi · Yongkang Street · Raohe Night Market

Zone: East Taipei
8:00am
Hike
Elephant Mountain (象山, Xiangshan) — MRT to Xiangshan station, Exit 2. Trail begins 5 minutes from the MRT.
Elephant Mountain Trail 象山步道
📍 Xinyi District ⏱ 20–30 min to main viewpoint 💰 Free 🥾 Beginner-friendly · steep stone stairs 🚇 Xiangshan MRT (Red Line)
Taipei's most iconic viewpoint — a steep but short climb of mostly stone stairs to the Six Boulders Lookout, where Taipei 101 and the city skyline unfold in front of you with almost theatrical effect. The photo most people have seen of Taipei is taken from here. Morning light is softer and crowd-free. To extend the experience and escape the tourists: continue past the main lookout to the Four Beasts scenic loop — connecting peaks Tiger, Lion, and Leopard Mountain, with ridge walking and city views in all directions. The loop adds 1–1.5 hours and the crowds drop to almost nothing within 10 minutes of the main viewpoint.
The stairs are genuinely steep despite being short — you'll feel it. Wear shoes with grip. Early morning (8–9am) is far quieter than late morning or sunset hours.
11:00am
Design District
Eslite Spectrum Songyan (誠品松菸) — a short walk from the trailhead or 1 MRT stop. Taiwan's flagship design bookstore, housed in a former tobacco factory, with curated indie clothing labels, concept home goods shops, craft studios, and a rooftop garden. Open 24 hours.
1:30pm
Neighborhood
Yongkang Street (永康街) and surrounding Da'an lanes — Taipei's most livable and artsy neighborhood. Independent cafés in old houses, design boutiques, bookshops, back-alley murals tucked into unexpected corners. Good lunch options (this is also where the famous Din Tai Fung flagship is, if you want the classic xiao long bao experience — the original location.
7:00pm
Night Market
Raohe Night Market (饒河夜市) — MRT to Songshan station, 1 stop.
Raohe Night Market 饒河街夜市
📍 Songshan District 🕐 Open daily ~5pm–midnight 🚇 Songshan MRT (Green Line)
A 600-meter covered street market stretching between two temple gates — one of Taipei's best night markets for actual eating rather than tourist spectacle. More local feel than Shilin. The anchor dish: black pepper buns (胡椒餅) from the famous oven stall at the temple entrance — thick, juicy pork buns baked in a clay oven, with a queue that tells you everything you need to know. Also look for: medicinal herb soup, braised pig's feet, stinky tofu (stronger than you think), fresh oyster omelets. The temple at the far end (Ciyou Temple) is beautiful to walk through and free.
The black pepper bun stall is at the Ciyou Temple entrance end. The queue is fast-moving. You want this.

Day 4 · Wed Nov 4

Yangmingshan Hike · Dihua Street · Dadaocheng Wharf Sunset · Ningxia

Wharf sunset added Zone: North → Old City West
8:00am
Hike
Yangmingshan National Park — Bus from Jiantan MRT (Red Line) direct to the park. Multiple bus routes available from the Jiantan bus stop.
Yangmingshan National Park 陽明山國家公園
📍 North Taipei ⏱ 2–4 hrs depending on trail 💰 Free 🥾 Beginner to intermediate
A volcanic national park sitting directly above Taipei's northern suburbs — sulfur fumaroles, hot springs, and extraordinary highland scenery. In November, the silver grass (芒草) is in full bloom across the hillsides, turning the landscape into something out of a Japanese film.

Recommended for your fitness level: The Erziping Trail (二子坪步道) — flat, accessible, 2-km loop through misty highland meadows with volcanic landscape and the famous sulfur fumaroles at Xiaoyoukeng nearby. Entirely different in character from Elephant Mountain — here you're above the clouds rather than looking up at a skyline.

For more challenge: Qixingshan (七星山, Seven Star Mountain) at 1,120m — the highest peak in Taipei, moderate 3-hr round trip with steep stone steps and 360° views. Stunning but commit to an early start.
Free foot-soaking hot spring pool at Lengshuikeng visitor center on the descent — natural geothermal water, wooden benches, local elderly crowd, completely free. One of the most pleasant 20 minutes in Taipei.
1:00pm
Market
Return to Jiantan MRT → Minquan W. Rd area → walk to Dihua Street (15-min walk from Minquan W. Rd station).
Dihua Street 迪化街 + Dadaocheng District
📍 Datong District — 15-min walk from Minquan W. Rd MRT 🕐 Most shops open 10am–6pm
Taipei's oldest commercial street, stretching along the Tamsui River in a neighborhood called Dadaocheng — once Taiwan's most important trade port. The Baroque shophouse facades (built by 19th-century merchants who wanted to look like they'd traveled the world) now house a wonderful mix: traditional dried goods and tea merchants operating exactly as they have for 150 years, alongside indie design pop-ups, concept cafés, and fabric stores. It's one of the few places in Taipei where you can feel the city's actual age. Browse at will — buy tea, dried mango, fabric if it interests you.
The small Xiahai City God Temple (霞海城隍廟) at the north end of Dihua St. is one of the most popular matchmaking temples in Taiwan — there's usually a long prayer line. Worth a quick look.
4:30pm
Hidden Gem
Dadaocheng Wharf / Pier 5 — walk 10 minutes west from Dihua Street down to the Tamsui River.
Dadaocheng Wharf 大稻埕碼頭 (Pier 5)
📍 West end of Dihua St., Tamsui River 🕐 Late afternoon into evening — food stalls open ~4:30pm 💰 Free
Underrated sunset spot in Taipei. From late afternoon the riverside pier transforms: bars, craft beer stalls, Taiwanese street food, live music, and locals gathering at the river's edge as the sky turns. The Tamsui River reflects the pink and orange light perfectly.
Budget 1.5–2 hours here. Bring a light jacket as it cools by the river after dark.
7:30pm
Night Market
Ningxia Night Market (寧夏夜市) — 15-minute walk south from the wharf. Taipei's most beloved local night market for traditional Taiwanese snacks: braised pork rice (the definitive version), oyster omelet, aiyu jelly with lemon, red bean cakes. Compact, never overwhelming, always delicious. Ends around midnight.

Jiufen Day Trip · Teapot Mountain · Yin-Yang Sea · Taboo Night

Self-contained loop
8:00am
Transit
Bus from Taipei Main Station to Jiufen Old Street (~1 hour). Bus 1062 or tour buses from the Taipei Bus Station. Returns run until late evening.
9:30am
Hike + Views
Short bus hop to Jinguashi → Teapot Mountain trail.
Teapot Mountain 茶壺山 · Jinguashi Gold Ecological Park
📍 Jinguashi, New Taipei City ⏱ 2–3 hrs total 💰 Free trail (park entrance free if hiking only) 🥾 Intermediate — involves rock scrambling
One of the most satisfying hikes near Taipei: the trail winds up through a former gold mining area to a distinctive cluster of rocks shaped exactly like a teapot, with a cave-like passage through the "spout" that you physically climb through. The views from the top look down on the coastline, the ruined mining infrastructure, and the extraordinary Yin-Yang Sea directly below.

The Yin-Yang Sea (陰陽海) is a natural phenomenon caused by iron sulfide seeping from the old mine drainage — the ocean turns a striking blue-gold at the point where the mineral water meets the sea. You'll see it clearly from the Teapot Mountain ridgeline and from the road below. It looks almost CGI. There's a viewpoint platform on the main road specifically for photographs.

The Jinguashi Gold Ecological Park itself is fascinating — the remains of a former Japanese-era gold mine with preserved infrastructure, a museum, and a free touchable gold bar (the largest single piece you can legally touch in the world at 220kg).
The cave passage through the "teapot" requires some scrambling and ducking — not technical, but not for anyone with claustrophobia. The longer Banpingshan extension (adds 1 hr, more scrambling, ropes on cliff sections) is spectacular if you want more challenge.
1:30pm
Shops
Return to Jiufen Old Street. The stepped alley market has some of the best small indie shops in northern Taiwan: handmade jewelry, ceramics, vintage accessories, prints. Also: warm taro balls (芋圓), peanut ice cream wraps, and the tea houses perched over the misty hillside above the sea — some of the most atmospheric settings for a cup of tea you'll find anywhere.
5:30pm
Return

9:30pm

City Two

Tainan

November 7–9 · 2 nights · arrive evening Nov 6

Taiwan's oldest city (400+ years) and undisputed food capital. Every street has a temple alley; every alley has a noodle stall. Tainan is where Taiwanese identity is most concentrated — the city has more temples per capita than anywhere in Taiwan, more distinct local dishes than any other city, and a creative, artsy energy that coexists naturally with its historical weight. Unlike Taipei, Tainan rewards slowness. The best moments are found by wandering: pushing through a door you weren't sure about, following lantern light down a lane, sitting at a plastic stool at midnight eating beef soup.

Stay: West Central District — walking distance to all main sights, Jazz Fest venue, temples, and Shennong St. Hotel Cozzi Tainan (mid-range, reliable) · Wabisabi Hotel (budget, Japanese capsule aesthetic, near Shennong St). NT$1,200–3,000/night.
Day 7 · Sat Nov 7

West Central Temples · Confucius + Narrow Door Café · Hai'an Road · Jazz Festival Night 2

🎷 Jazz Fest Night 2 Confucius Temple + Café added Zone: Walkable cluster
7:30am
Breakfast
Tainan beef soup — locals queue from 6am for this. A bowl of clear beef broth with paper-thin slices of raw beef that cook in the hot soup, served with rice or vermicelli. One of Tainan's most distinctive dishes. Look for any stall with a crowd near the train station area — A-Cai Beef Soup (阿財牛肉湯) is a well-known option. This sets the tone for Tainan: food as ritual.
9:00am
Temples
West Central temple walk — three major temples all within a 10-minute walking loop of each other.
Chihkan Tower 赤崁樓 (Fort Provintia)
📍 West Central District🕐 8:30am–9pm💰 NT$50
Built by the Dutch in 1653 as Fort Provintia, now completely Chinese in architectural style after centuries of rebuilds. The complex has two main buildings connected by a bridge, beautiful koi ponds, old stone tablets, and nine stone turtle statues carrying imperial stelae (rubbing the turtles' heads is said to bring good luck). Lit up beautifully at night — the tower stays open until 9pm.
God of War Temple 武廟 · Long Crimson Wall
📍 2-min walk from Chihkan Tower💰 Free
The most photogenic exterior in Tainan: a long, continuous crimson-red wall running the full length of the street, flanking the temple entrance. One of the most visually striking urban facades in all of Taiwan — photographers come specifically for this wall at golden hour.
Grand Matsu Temple 大天后宮
📍 2-min walk💰 Free
Originally the palace of a Ming Dynasty prince who committed suicide when the Qing took over. The building was converted into a Matsu temple — the most important Matsu shrine in southern Taiwan. The large Matsu statue on the main altar was carved 300 years ago by a master from Quanzhou and is considered a masterpiece of religious sculpture.
10:30am
Cultural Landmark
Confucius Temple and Narrow Door Café — a 10-minute walk from the God of War Temple.
Tainan Confucius Temple 台南孔廟 (Taiwan's Oldest)
📍 West Central District🕐 8:30am–5:30pm💰 Free (main hall)
Built in 1665 — the oldest Confucius Temple in Taiwan, predating the one in Taipei by over 70 years. Serene, uncrowded scholarly courtyards with old trees, stone pavings, and the quiet dignity of a place that has been visited by students seeking blessing for centuries. Free to enter the main hall. Small fee for the inner palace. Worth 30–45 minutes.
Narrow Door Café 窄門咖啡
📍 Directly across from the Confucius Temple🕐 ~10am–10pm
One of Tainan's most famous cafés and most delightful experiences: you enter by squeezing sideways through a 38cm-wide corridor between two buildings. Inside is an old mansion with soaring ceilings, bold colors, mismatched vintage furniture, and genuinely excellent coffee. The entrance alone is worth the visit — the coffee makes it worth staying. Queer-friendly atmosphere, artistic crowd. Decaffeinated options available if you're coffeed out.
You will feel ridiculous squeezing through. Everyone does. That's the point.
12:00pm
Art Museum
Tainan Art Museum Building 1 — directly next to the Confucius Temple. A converted 1931 Japanese-era Art Deco police station, now an art museum where the building itself is as interesting as the exhibitions. Two buildings total (Building 2 is a striking modern structure in the city center); Building 1's historic shell is the one to prioritize. Contemporary Taiwanese and international shows.
2:00pm
Public Art Street
Hai'an Road Art Street (海安路) — a 10-minute walk from the Art Museum.
Hai'an Road Art Street 海安路藝術街
📍 West Central District💰 Free, outdoor
A major city thoroughfare transformed into an open-air public art corridor: large-scale murals painted directly onto the façades of buildings along the full length of the road, interspersed with light installations, sculptural streetscapes, and creative interventions in the public space. Some installations are purely visual; others are interactive. Best visited at dusk when the pieces light up and the whole street takes on a different character. This is Tainan's most concentrated outdoor art space — and one of the best street art environments in Taiwan.
The installations light up after dark — if you pass back through this area on the way to the Jazz Festival, the evening version is completely different in mood.
4:00pm
Design
Blueprint Cultural Creative Park (藍曬圖文創園區) nearby — former military dependents' village now covered in outdoor murals and housing indie design studios, pop-up shops, and weekend vendor markets. Hayashi Department Store (台南林百貨) — stunning 1930s Japanese Art Deco six-story department store, the only pre-war department store still operating in Taiwan. Curated Taiwanese design goods, local snacks, and an extraordinary rooftop Shinto shrine (the only one still standing above a department store in Asia). Free to browse.
7:30pm
Festival
Tainan Jazz Festival — Final Night at Wu Garden. Second nights of music festivals typically have the most energy — the performers have warmed up, the crowd knows what it's doing. Walk to the venue from your hotel. If you want to split the evening: Jazz Festival until 10pm, then taxi to Garden Night Market (NT$150) which runs until midnight on Saturdays.

Nature · Must-See

Sicao Green Tunnel 四草綠色隧道 — "Taiwan's Little Amazon"
📍 Annan District, Tainan (Sicao Dazhong Temple) 🕐 Open daily 8am–4pm 💰 NT$200 per person (Mangrove Tunnel route) ⏱ 30–40 min boat ride + 30 min visit time
One of the most remarkable natural experiences in southern Taiwan, and a genuine gap in most Tainan itineraries. A bamboo raft glides through a waterway where four species of 50-year-old native mangroves have grown together overhead, their branches interlocking to form a dense, cathedral-like green canopy. The light filters through in patterns; egrets and black-crowned night herons fly alongside at eye level; fiddler crabs move through the roots. At the tunnel's deepest point, the still water creates a perfect mirror reflection of the canopy — an optical illusion called the "Angel's Kiss" or "Green Eye" where the boundary between tree and reflection disappears entirely.

The experience is genuinely transportive. Guides narrate (mostly Mandarin, but the biology signs are in English and the visual experience speaks for itself). The adjacent free Sperm Whale Museum — entered with your ticket stub — houses complete specimens of a stranded mother and calf sperm whale that beached nearby. Unexpected and genuinely moving.
Go at 8am opening: cooler, fewer people, and the morning light through the canopy is extraordinary. Bring bug spray. The guides are enthusiastic and knowledgeable — the boat commentary is worth a translation attempt through your phone if you're curious.
This is part of Taijiang National Park (台江國家公園) — one of Taiwan's least-visited national parks despite being extraordinary. The wetlands protect over 240 species of fish, 200+ shellfish species, and are critical habitat for the endangered black-faced spoonbill which winters here Nov–March. You may see them from the boat.

Colonial History
Anping Fort + Anping Treehouse.
Anping Fort 安平古堡 (Fort Zeelandia, 1624)
📍 Anping District🕐 8:30am–5:30pm💰 NT$70
Taiwan's oldest surviving fort, built by the Dutch East India Company in 1624 as their regional headquarters. A 65-meter section of the original 17th-century red brick wall still stands — arguably the most significant Dutch colonial relic you can see in Taiwan. The attached museum has old maps, trade records, and the story of Koxinga — the half-Chinese, half-Japanese Ming loyalist who drove out the Dutch in 1662 and made Taiwan his base. Climb the observation tower for harbor views.
Anping Treehouse 安平樹屋
📍 5-min walk from Anping Fort🕐 8:30am–5:30pm💰 NT$70
The most visually extraordinary site in Tainan. A 19th-century salt trading warehouse belonging to the Tait & Co. merchant house was abandoned, and over the following decades the roots and branches of surrounding banyan trees grew through and around it, completely consuming the structure. Today, elevated walkways thread through the tangled root system — banyan roots the width of your torso wrap around walls, push through roofs, and cascade down facades. It looks like something from a Studio Ghibli film. Eerie, beautiful, genuinely unlike anything else. Allow 45 minutes.
Often compared to Angkor Wat's Ta Prohm temple for the tree-consuming-ruin aesthetic. This is genuinely one of the most unique architectural sights in Taiwan.

Tofu pudding (豆花). Prawn crackers. This is Tainan street eating at its most traditional.

City Three

Kaohsiung

November 9–12 · 3 nights

Taiwan's second city is a port town that has reinvented itself through public art and creative culture without losing its working-class harbor soul. Pier 2 — a series of decommissioned warehouses along the waterfront — anchors a creative district that has become the best example of industrial regeneration in Taiwan. The city is also home to wild macaques hiking trails, a dramatic British-era clifftop consulate, and some of the most photogenic religious architecture in the south. The MRT is a single straight north-south Red Line, making it easier to navigate than Taipei.

Stay: Near Formosa Boulevard MRT (Red Line) · Hotel Dua or Just Sleep Kaohsiung (mid-range, design sensibility, NT$2,000–4,000) · Original Hostel near Pier 2 (budget, social). All within 10 min of Pier 2, Liuhe Night Market, and Love River.

Former British Consulate at Takao 打狗英國領事館
📍 Top of the hill above Gushan Ferry Pier 🕐 Tue–Sun 10am–6pm (Mon closed) 💰 NT$99
The most dramatic viewpoint in Kaohsiung. A 1865 red brick building — the oldest Western building in Taiwan — sits on a cliff 50 meters above the harbor mouth, looking out over the Taiwan Strait, Cijin Island, and the entire Kaohsiung port. Built as the British consular residence when Kaohsiung (then called Takao) was a key treaty port, it has the scale and position of a Mediterranean fortress. Restored and open for tours, with period furniture and trade history exhibits. The view from the terrace at sunset is exceptional.
Walk up the steps from Gushan Ferry Pier — about 10 minutes uphill. Don't take a taxi; the approach on foot is part of the drama.

Cijin Island 旗津
📍 10-min ferry from Gushan Pier 💰 Ferry NT$30
A narrow sand-spit island enclosing Kaohsiung harbor — once the city's oldest settlement, now a relaxed half-day escape. Cihou Fort at the north tip (1875, Qing-era cannon emplacements, harbor views). A long beachfront walk along the west coast facing the open Taiwan Strait. The central lane of seafood restaurants is where locals come for the freshest harbor catch — oysters, sea bass, clams — served at plastic tables with cold Taiwan Beer. Rent a bicycle from the ferry terminal (NT$100/hr) to see the full island in under an hour.
The Cijin seafood restaurants on the main lane are excellent and cheap. Order by pointing — the fish tanks are labeled in Chinese but the staff will show you options. A full lunch for two runs NT$400–700.

Shoushan Monkey Mountain · Yancheng Arts District · Love River · Lotus Lake

Shoushan — must-add Kaohsiung hike Zone: West → North Kaohsiung
7:30am
Hike · Wildlife
Early start for Shoushan (壽山) — Monkey Mountain. Bus 56 from Wunwu Shengdian LRT stop, or taxi to Shoushan Zoo entrance (NT$150).
Shoushan Nature Park 壽山自然公園 ("Monkey Mountain")
📍 West Kaohsiung — directly above the harbor ⏱ 2–3 hrs depending on route 💰 Free 🥾 Moderate — steep in sections, mostly well-paved
Kaohsiung's best nature experience and the hike that most visitors miss entirely. Shoushan is a limestone ridge rising directly out of the city on its western edge, home to a large population of wild Formosan rock macaques — Taiwan's only native primate — that roam freely along every trail. You will walk alongside troops of them: mothers with infants, juveniles playing in the trees, elders sitting on boulders surveying the trail with complete indifference to your presence. This kind of proximity to wild primates in an urban setting is genuinely unusual anywhere in the world.

From the ridgeline, views open over the entire Kaohsiung harbor, the Love River delta, Lotus Pond to the north, and on clear days the Taiwan Strait to the west. The contrast of wild forest and working port city below is completely distinctive to Kaohsiung.

Recommended route: Enter from the Shoushan Zoo (zoo entrance, trail continues past the zoo gate into the mountain). The main Zhongzheng Hiking Trail reaches the ridgeline in about 40 minutes. Turn north along the ridge for the harbor views. Return by the same path or loop via Chaishan Road on the east slope.
⚠️ Do NOT carry visible food. The macaques are experienced thieves and move fast. Keep snacks deep in your bag and zipped. They will not harm you if you don't have food — they're completely habituated to hikers. Feed them at your own risk (it's also bad for them ecologically).
Go early (7:30–8am) before the heat builds. November is comfortable but the limestone can be slippery after the rare rain. The morning light through the forest is beautiful.
11:00am

Yancheng District 鹽埕區 + Dagouding Old Street
📍 Base of Shoushan, West Kaohsiung
Kaohsiung's oldest commercial neighborhood — the Yancheng 1st Public Market (a historic wet market still operating as it has for 70+ years) is surrounded by a creative cluster that has grown around it: street art on every available surface, vintage shops in old market buildings, independent record stores, and small design studios. Dagouding Old Street (打狗鐵道故事館 area) has a few preserved Japanese-era buildings and a casual arts vibe without the Pier 2 crowds. Far more neighborhood-scale than anywhere else in Kaohsiung — feels genuinely lived-in.

Temple Complex
MRT Red Line north to Zuoying → Lotus Lake.
Lotus Lake 蓮池潭 — Dragon & Tiger Pagodas
📍 Zuoying District, north Kaohsiung 🕐 Pagodas open daily 8am–5pm 💰 Free ⚠️ Dragon & Tiger Pagodas fully reopened 2025 after renovation
A man-made lake ringed by brightly colored temples, pavilions, and religious architecture of maximum visual intensity. The anchor: the Dragon and Tiger Pagodas — two seven-story towers rising from the water, one a dragon (enter through its open mouth), one a tiger (exit through its mouth), with elaborate hand-painted murals inside depicting heaven and hell in vivid detail. Kaohsiung folk religion at its most theatrical.

The Spring and Autumn Pavilions nearby (connected to the lake by a zigzag bridge) house a famous Xuantian Supreme Emperor statue and sit over the water on painted causeway bridges. The whole complex is maximalist religious art at a scale you won't find anywhere else — it rewards wandering and looking closely at the painted detail rather than rushing through for photos.
Allow 1.5 hours minimum for the full circuit. Enter the Dragon's mouth, walk the murals inside, exit the Tiger — this is the traditional sequence said to transform bad luck into good. Even if you're skeptical, the murals alone are worth it.

Weiwuying National Kaohsiung Center for the Arts 衛武營國家藝術文化中心
📍 Fengshan District (on the LRT/MRT Red Line) 🕐 Tue–Sun 11am–8pm (grounds accessible from morning) 💰 Free to wander
The largest performing arts center in Southeast Asia, designed by Dutch firm Mecanoo (2018). The building's form was inspired by the banyan trees of the surrounding park — its roof undulates in organic waves, creating open-air passages and public plazas that flow continuously between inside and outside without walls. You can walk through the entire complex for free, past the opera house, concert hall, and theater lobbies. The building itself is a work of public art: bold, strange, and completely unlike any performing arts building you've seen. Check the website for any morning performances or outdoor markets during your visit.
The surrounding park (former military grounds, now beautifully landscaped) is excellent for a morning walk before heading north.

Industrial Heritage
MRT Red Line one stop north → Ciaotou Refinery station → Qiaotou Sugar Factory.
Qiaotou Sugar Factory 橋頭糖廠
📍 Qiaotou District — Ciaotou Refinery MRT Station 🕐 Open daily 9am–5pm 💰 Free
Taiwan's oldest sugar refinery (1901, built by the Japanese colonial administration) operated continuously until 1999 when the last crop was processed and the factory went silent. Now an industrial heritage park, entirely free to explore: original narrow-gauge railway carriages on rusting tracks through the grounds, factory buildings in varying states of photogenic decay, koi ponds, old colonial staff housing converted to small shops, and public art installations scattered throughout. Guerrilla murals appear in every corner — the peeling paint, aged metal, and overgrown grounds make it one of the most atmosphere-rich spaces in Kaohsiung.

Mikan the station cat — the resident station cat, adopted by MRT staff and now a minor celebrity with a cat-themed bakery next to the MRT exit and a proper station employee badge. Worth five minutes of your day purely for the delight of it.
Best in morning light when the long shadows fall across the old railway tracks. The ice cream stall sells sugarcane-flavored soft serve made from the refinery's last batch of sugar — iconic.

Guandu Temple 關渡宮
📍 Guandu, Beitou District 🕐 Open daily 6am–9pm 💰 Free 🚇 Guandu MRT (Red Line, end of the Xinbeitou branch)
One of the most dramatically situated temples in all of Taiwan — a 1661 Matsu temple built directly into the face of a cliff above the Tamsui River, where the river bends toward the sea. The main temple hall fronts the water; behind it, a winding cave tunnel carved into the rock interior passes through a series of small shrines, altars, and stone-carved reliefs in near-darkness before emerging on the other side of the cliff into a manicured garden with pruned trees and harbor views below. The transition from sunlit incense-filled temple courtyard into a rock-carved underground passage into a secret cliff garden is one of the most surprising architectural sequences in Taipei.

Guandu Wharf is a 10-minute walk downhill from the temple — a quiet riverside pier with morning food stalls, bamboo fishing rafts, and views over the Tamsui River wetlands toward the sea. Very local, very unhurried.
Walk through the cave passage even if you're not religious — the transition from the incense-smoky outer hall through the carved rock interior is extraordinary. Allow 45 minutes for the full temple complex and 30 minutes at the wharf.
